# Architecture 5 – Implicit Security Assumptions (GPT-4o, Fresh Run)

Assumption 1: Finance users are properly authenticated

Why it matters:
The ERP relies on accurate user identity before any SOX controls can function.

If false:
Unauthorized individuals may create, modify, or approve financial records.

⸻

Assumption 2: User credentials are protected

Why it matters:
Identity is the foundation of accountability.

If false:
Attackers may impersonate finance personnel.

⸻

Assumption 3: MFA or equivalent protections exist

Why it matters:
The architecture does not explicitly mention MFA.

If false:
Credential theft may lead directly to ERP access.

⸻

Assumption 4: Session management is secure

Why it matters:
Authenticated sessions represent user identity.

If false:
Session hijacking may bypass authentication controls.

⸻

Assumption 5: Session identifiers are unpredictable

Why it matters:
Weak session tokens can be guessed.

If false:
Unauthorized users may assume valid sessions.

⸻

Assumption 6: Authorization is implemented correctly

Why it matters:
Authentication alone does not enforce financial controls.

If false:
Users may perform unauthorized actions.

⸻

Assumption 7: User roles accurately reflect job responsibilities

Why it matters:

If false:
Users may receive excessive permissions.

⸻

Assumption 8: Role assignments are reviewed regularly

Why it matters:

If false:
Excessive access may persist undetected.

⸻

Assumption 9: User provisioning is accurate

Why it matters:
Access must align with business functions.

If false:
Unauthorized users may gain ERP access.

⸻

Assumption 10: User deprovisioning occurs promptly

⸻

Assumption 11: Segregation-of-duties rules are correctly defined

Why it matters:
SOX controls depend on proper separation of responsibilities.

If false:
Single users may gain excessive financial authority.

⸻

Assumption 12: Segregation-of-duties controls are technically enforced
